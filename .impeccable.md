## Design Context

### Users
This site is for Akshay's own full-stack developer portfolio. The primary audience is founders, teams, collaborators, and recruiters evaluating whether he can build dependable software and think clearly about product delivery. Their job to be done is to quickly assess engineering depth, shipped work, product judgment, and whether Akshay feels like the right person to collaborate with.

### Brand Personality
Builder. Systems. Curious.

The tone should feel trustworthy, promising, thoughtful, and technically sharp without becoming cold or corporate. The interface should communicate calm confidence and delivery maturity rather than visual noise or trend-chasing.

### Aesthetic Direction
The site should function as a developer portfolio with a minimal palette, low clutter, and clear information hierarchy. It should work in both light and dark themes. The visual language should avoid clunky layouts, excessive card usage, and decorative complexity. Existing installed packages should be preferred when introducing new UI pieces.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with proof: every section should make shipped work, technical judgment, and delivery clarity obvious within a few seconds.
2. Keep the system restrained: use fewer colors, fewer surfaces, and stronger hierarchy instead of piling on effects.
3. Show systems thinking: emphasize process, product judgment, and engineering depth rather than abstract service language.
4. Stay curious but disciplined: allow a few thoughtful interaction details, but avoid clutter and novelty for its own sake.
5. Support both themes intentionally: light and dark modes should each feel designed, not inverted by accident.
